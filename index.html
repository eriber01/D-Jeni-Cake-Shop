Strawberry Cake

Strawberry shortcake is one of the classic fruit tarts that is made in many shapes and whose top layer is usually made of strawberries. It is not only known in Germanic countries but also in England, Poland and Russia.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 Cups of Cream
  • 3 Cups of Flour
  • 1/2 Sugar Cups
  • 1 1/2 Teaspoons of Baking Powder
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 2 sticks of unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 teaspoons liquid vanilla
  • 5 cups strawberry, sliced
  • 3 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • vanilla frosting

Preparation

  • Pre-heat the oven. In a bowl mix the flour, three tablespoons of sugar, the baking powder and the pinch of salt. Add the butter in small pieces.
  • Mix with a fork in a bowl 3/4 cup of cream, the eggs, the vanilla and the flour mixture. Just until ingredients are wet, not over gowns.
  • Take the dough and form a circle, put it on a baking sheet. Add 2 tablespoons of cream and sprinkle with a tablespoon of sugar.
  • Bake until golden brown for about 25 minutes. Let it cool completely.
  • Marinate the strawberries with the remaining quarter cup of sugar for an hour.
  • With an electric mixer, beat the cream with the remaining sugar to the point of nougat until it forms peaks. Once the cake is completely cold, cut it in half add the cream and the sliced strawberries. Cover again with the other layer of cake and on top put the vanilla bitumen and the mixture of macerated strawberries.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams of cream cheese
  • 200 grams of sugar
  • 500 milliliters of cream, to whip 35% fat
  • 200 milliliters of cow's milk
  • 2 teaspoons liquid vanilla
  • 2 curd envelopes
  • 1 chocolate cookie package
  • al taste of caramel

Preparation

  • Beat the sugar with the cheese, making sure they are mixed well.
  • In a glass place about 100 milliliters of milk and dissolve the two sachets of curd.
  • Place the cream, the rest of the milk, the vanilla and the cheese with sugar that we mixed before, in a saucepan over low heat. When it boils, lower the heat and add the curd. Mix and bring to a boil, with the heat as low as possible, wait a few minutes for the mixture to thicken. After a while, turn off the heat and remove the saucepan.
  • While you wait for it to temper a bit, take advantage and mount the base. Place all the chocolate cookies that fit whole in a mold and then fill the holes with pieces. Try to fill in as much as possible.
  • Pour the cream cheese over the cookie and wait for it to cool enough before putting it in the fridge.
  • Finally, add the liquid caramel and leave the cake without unmolding until the moment of serving.
